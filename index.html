Sediment transport modeling from upstream reaches of Galveston Bay during Hurricane Harvey

Course Project Fall 2023

Hurricane Harvey

  • Category 4 hurricane
  • Highest winds: 130 mph (215 km/h)
  • Lowest pressure: 937 mbar
  • Damage: $125 billion (2017 USD)

Galveston Bay



  • Seventh largest estuary in the US
  • A complex mixture of sea water and fresh water





Why Harvey so serious?

  • Micro-tidal meteorologically dominant bay, meteorologically dominate bay, sediment resuspension and transport results from cold fronts
  • Sediment resuspension and transport result from cold fronts
  • High withdrawal rate of groundwater resulted in elevated land subsidence
  • Harvey was a slow-moving tropical cyclone with the potential to deliver substantially greater volumes of floodwaters










Motivation

  • Harvey deposited 131.34 × 106 tons of sediment, representing 31 years of annual sediment load in the bay
  • It also resulted in the erosion of 16.4 × 106 tons of sediment, containing at least 3.43 tons of Hg.

Research Goals

  • Modeling the influences of Buffalo Bayou and San Jacinto River on sediment dynamics during Harvey
  • Estimate total erosion and deposition masses
  • Compare deposition patterns: model vs. sediment core data

Define & discretize the domain

  • San Jacinto Estuary + upper Galveston Bay
  • High resolution resolving the deep Houston Shipping Channel and Buffalo Bayou (width of 160-300 m)

Calculate variables

e.g., velocity, sediment concentration

Temporal & Spatial dimension

  • Initial conditions: defining the state at first timestep
    • 3D momentum, salinity, temperature: from a parent SCHISM model
    • Sediment bed properties: Texas Coastal Sediments Geodatabase
  • Boundary conditions
    • River discharge: National Water Model
    • River sediment: USGS Texas Real-Time Water Quality
    • Open boundary conditions: from a larger-scale parent ROMS model (~100 m resolution)

Model simulation

Time:08/05/2017 ~ 09/15/2017
Analysis period: 08/25 ~ 09/05
