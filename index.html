Sediment Mobilization from Upstream Reaches of Galveston Bay During Hurricane Harvey

Course Project Fall 2023

Background

Harvey brought record-breaking precipitation to the Galveston Bay area

Significant Flooding

  • Two main sources of land-derived runoff
  • Storm surge and an anomalously high baseline in sea level from the ocean compounded flooding
  • BB, SJR, and oceanic forcings comprised the triple punch

Research Goals

  • Modeling the influences of Buffalo Bayou and San Jacinto River on sediment dynamics during Harvey
  • Estimate total erosion and deposition masses
  • Compare deposition patterns: model vs. sediment core data

Define & discretize the domain

  • San Jacinto Estuary + upper Galveston Bay
  • High resolution resolving the deep Houston Shipping Channel and Buffalo Bayou (width of 160-300 m)

Calculate variables

e.g., velocity, sediment concentration

Temporal & Spatial dimension

  • Initial conditions: defining the state at first timestep
    • 3D momentum, salinity, temperature: from a parent SCHISM model
    • Sediment bed properties: Texas Coastal Sediments Geodatabase
  • Boundary conditions
    • River discharge: National Water Model
    • River sediment: USGS Texas Real-Time Water Quality
    • Open boundary conditions: from a larger-scale parent ROMS model (~100 m resolution)

Model simulation

Time:08/05/2017 ~ 09/15/2017
Analysis period: 08/25 ~ 09/05
